# Video 81 — What the CPA Exam Is — Do You Need It?
**Chunk:** 81 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 3 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** What the CPA is, the four sections, who needs it, the cost and timeline — and whether you should start it in year one.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** CPA — Certified Public Accountant. The credential is issued by state boards and administered through the AICPA. It's the gatekeeper for signing audit reports and filing with the SEC.

**0:20 —** Four exam sections under the 2024 structure: FAR (Financial Accounting & Reporting), AUD (Auditing), REG (Regulation/Tax), and BAR or ISC (Business Analysis & Reporting, or Information Systems & Controls).

**0:55 —** Who needs it: public accounting (audit, tax) — generally required for advancement. Industry accounting — often not required but valued, especially as you move up.

**1:20 —** Timeline and cost: typically 18 months to complete all four sections, ~$3,000–$5,000 in exam fees plus prep materials. There's a 150-credit-hour education requirement in most states beyond the bachelor's degree.

**1:50 —** The real question: do you want public accounting or industry? That answer decides whether the CPA is essential or optional. Strong advice regardless: even if you don't need it, start FAR in year one. It reinforces exactly what this course covered — GAAP, the accounting cycle, financial statements, revenue recognition. Studying FAR makes you a better staff accountant immediately, not just a future CPA.

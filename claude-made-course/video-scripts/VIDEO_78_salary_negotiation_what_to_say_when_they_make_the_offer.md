# Video 78 — Salary Negotiation — What to Say When They Make the Offer
**Chunk:** 78 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 3.5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Role-play through the three salary moments — application form, verbal offer, and benefits — with the exact counter-offer phrase word for word.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** Salary negotiation happens in three moments, and the words differ in each. (Interviewer off-screen voice; candidate text on screen.)

**0:15 —** Moment 1, the application form "What are your salary expectations?": "I'm open to competitive compensation for this role and location. Based on my research the range for entry-level staff accountants in [city] is $X–$Y, and I'm flexible within that range based on the full package." If you can leave it blank, do.

**1:10 —** Moment 2, the verbal offer — do NOT accept on the spot: "Thank you — I'm very excited about this opportunity. Can I take 48 hours to review the details?" Then the counter-offer, word for word: "Based on my research and the full scope of the role, I was hoping for [10–15% above their number]. Is there flexibility there?" Say the number — vague counters get vague responses.

**2:10 —** If they say no: "Could we revisit compensation after 6 months if my performance meets expectations?" — and get that in the offer letter.

**2:45 —** Moment 3, when salary is fixed — negotiate benefits: remote work days, a CPA/professional-development budget, start-date flexibility, a sign-on bonus if relocation is required.

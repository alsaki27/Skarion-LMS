# Video 102 — What Happens During an Audit — From the Accountant's Seat
**Chunk:** 102 · **Module:** Module 14 · **Duration target:** 4 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Auditors aren't looking for what you did wrong — they're hired to verify you got it right. Know what they test and audit prep becomes organizing work you already did.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** The first time auditors walk in, it feels like a tax audit — like they're looking for something you did wrong. They're not. Financial statement auditors are hired by the company's board to verify the numbers are fairly presented. They want to help you prove your numbers are right.

**0:30 —** What auditors actually test: **existence** (does the asset exist?), **completeness** (are all transactions recorded?), **accuracy** (are the amounts correct?), **cutoff** (are things in the right period?), and **valuation** (are things valued correctly?). For each assertion, they select a sample of transactions and trace them to source documents.

**1:30 —** What an accountant prepares for audit: a complete reconciliation package (every balance sheet account tied to its subledger), a depreciation schedule with asset receipts attached, the payroll summary tied to W-2s, and all adjusting entries with approval documentation. If you've done the close properly every month, audit prep is just organizing what you already have.

**3:00 —** The most common audit finding for small companies: *missing supporting documentation*. The fix: every journal entry, every adjustment, every unusual transaction — there's a document attached *before* you post it. Not after. Before.

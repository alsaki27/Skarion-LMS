# Video 33 — How to Read a Chart of Accounts in 5 Minutes
**Chunk:** 33 · **Module:** Module 02 · **Duration target:** 3.5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** The Chart of Accounts is the master list of every bucket money can go into. If you don't understand it, you can't do the job.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** The Chart of Accounts is the master list of every bucket that money can go into at a company. Every journal entry, every financial statement, every report — all of it traces back to this list.

**0:25 —** Here is the pattern that never changes: accounts starting with 1 are Assets — things the company owns. 2s are Liabilities — things the company owes. 3s are Equity. 4s are Revenue. And 5 through 6 are Expenses. This numbering is not arbitrary — it maps directly to the accounting equation.

**1:15 —** Let me show you Skarion Engineering's actual Chart of Accounts... [walks through 1000 Operating Checking, 1200 Unbilled AR (WIP), 2100 Accrued Wages, 5100 COGS — Subcontractor Drafting, explaining each]. Notice 1200 is an asset even though nothing's been billed yet — that's WIP, work already performed but not yet invoiced.

**2:30 —** One thing interviewers always ask: what's the difference between a temporary and a permanent account? Permanent accounts — Assets, Liabilities, Equity — carry their balance forward year after year. Temporary accounts — Revenue and Expenses — get zeroed out at year-end and their net balance flows into Retained Earnings.

**3:15 —** In the next section, you will do a drag-and-drop exercise with Skarion's actual transactions. Let's see if you can classify them correctly.

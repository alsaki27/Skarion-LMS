# Video 38 — How to Answer Any Accounting Interview Question
**Chunk:** 38 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 4 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Every candidate in your interview knows basic accounting. The real question is whether you can tell a specific story from real work that proves it.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** Every candidate in your interview knows basic accounting. The question the interviewer is actually asking is: can you tell me a specific story from real work that proves it? That's what separates hires from rejections.

**0:30 —** The framework is Context, Action, Result. Context: what company, what month, what situation. Action: what you did, what you checked, what you found. Result: the correction, the effect, the lesson. Every answer fits this structure.

**1:30 —** Here's the weak answer to "Tell me about a time you found an error in the books": "I'm very detail-oriented and I always catch errors." That's a motivational poster. Here's the strong answer: "During a bank reconciliation in January, the GL showed more cash than the bank statement. I compared deposits by date and amount, found a customer payment — $10,547.10 — entered twice. I confirmed the bank only received it once, reversed the duplicate with a Dr AR / Cr Cash, and the reconciliation balanced to $336,250." The second answer is unfakeable.

**2:30 —** Three things make it strong: a specific dollar amount, the company and document named, and the journal-entry direction exactly right — Dr AR not Sales Revenue, because the sale was never double-counted. That level of detail proves you actually did the work.

**3:30 —** In the interview prep module, you'll get 10 must-know questions with Skarion-specific answers. Practice out loud. The goal isn't to memorize — it's to internalize the structure so you can apply it to any question.

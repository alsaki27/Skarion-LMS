# Video 82 — Building Your Accounting Portfolio Before Your First Job
**Chunk:** 82 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 4 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** An accounting portfolio isn't a design portfolio — it's a document that proves you did real work. Here's what to include and where to keep it.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** An accounting portfolio isn't visual design. It's a document that proves you did real work. Here's what to include.

**0:15 —** 1) A one-page simulation summary: "I performed the following tasks for Skarion Engineering's January–March close cycle" — bullet points and specific dollar figures.

**0:45 —** 2) Screenshots of completed reconciliations — with numbers visible, company name anonymized if needed. 3) A completed financial statement package (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow) produced during the course. 4) Your STAR answer document — the 8 questions written out with specific numbers. 5) Your salary research notes — shows you think strategically, not just technically.

**1:40 —** Where to keep it: a PDF linked from your LinkedIn Featured section; a Notion page with embedded tables and screenshots; a GitHub repo if you're targeting tech-forward firms (shows comfort with documentation tools).

**2:10 —** The one sentence that kills interviews: "I don't have anything to show you yet." You do. It's this course. Present it like the work it is.

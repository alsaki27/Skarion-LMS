# Video 80 — Your First Week on the Job — Don't Do These Things
**Chunk:** 80 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 3.5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Five moves that tank your reputation in week one, and five that build a great one.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** Your reputation in a new accounting job is mostly set in the first week. Five things new hires do that tank it immediately:

**0:15 —** 1) Post an entry without checking with the manager first. 2) Assume you know the company's system because it looks like one you've seen. 3) Fix a mistake quietly instead of disclosing it. 4) Ask the same question twice without writing down the answer the first time. 5) Miss a deadline and not flag it until it's already late.

**1:30 —** Five things that build a great reputation in week one:

**1:40 —** 1) Ask to see last month's close package and read every entry. 2) Build your own COA cheat sheet on Day 1. 3) Confirm every task assignment in writing with the due date. 4) Never send anything without reviewing it twice. 5) Send a Friday wrap-up email to your manager — what you completed and what's pending.

**2:50 —** The theme: be a low-risk, high-clarity presence. Documentation beats talent in the first month, because trust isn't built from brilliance — it's built from not being a surprise.

# Video 76 — The STAR Method in 4 Minutes — With Real Accounting Examples
**Chunk:** 76 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 4 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Every behavioral question tests one thing: a specific, believable story with a real outcome. STAR is the structure that forces you to do that.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** Every behavioral interview question is a test of one thing: can you give me a specific, believable story with a real outcome? The STAR method forces you to.

**0:20 —** Situation: the context — who, what, where, when, one to two sentences. Task: what you were specifically responsible for. Action: the steps YOU took, not the team, not your manager. Result: what happened because of your actions — numbers if possible.

**1:00 —** Weak version of "found an error in the books": "I found an error and fixed it." That's not a story. Strong version: during January's bank rec, QuickBooks showed $34,000 more cash than the bank — Situation. My task was to trace it before close — Task. I compared every deposit by date and amount, found a $10,547.10 Southeast Broadband payment entered twice, confirmed the bank received it once, posted Dr AR / Cr Cash — Action. The rec balanced to $336,250 — Result.

**2:30 —** Notice it takes 45 seconds, names a real number, company, and journal entry. It doesn't say "I'm detail-oriented" — it proves it. That's the only thing that works in a technical interview.

**3:30 —** Your job: take the 8 questions in the next section and write STAR answers with your Skarion numbers. Don't memorize — internalize the structure.

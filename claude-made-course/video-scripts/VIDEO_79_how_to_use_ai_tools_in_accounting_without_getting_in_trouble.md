# Video 79 — How to Use AI Tools in Accounting — Without Getting in Trouble
**Chunk:** 79 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 4 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** AI handles the volume layer; you handle the judgment layer. The safe interview answer learns the line.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** Every accounting firm now uses some form of AI. The question isn't whether you'll work alongside it — it's whether you understand what it can and can't do.

**0:30 —** What AI is genuinely good at now: categorizing transactions, matching invoices to POs at scale, flagging anomalies in large GL exports, auto-generating first-draft journal-entry memos. Five thousand expense lines mapped to GL accounts — AI does that faster than any human.

**1:30 —** What AI is not good at: judgment on anything context-dependent. Does the expense belong in this period? Is this really an asset or an expense? Is this discount legitimately earned? AI doesn't know your policies, your contract terms, or what your CFO cares about. You do.

**2:30 —** The interview answer: "I use AI tools for the volume layer — categorization, anomaly flagging, first-draft memos. But every AI output gets reviewed before it hits the ledger. The AI suggests, I verify, I post." That shows you're current AND you understand that responsibility can't be delegated to software.

**3:30 —** Practically: learn ChatGPT for reconciliation-memo drafts, Excel Copilot for SUMIFS formulas, and how to spot when an AI-assigned category is wrong. That combination makes you more productive, not replaceable.

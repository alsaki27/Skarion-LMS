# Video 41 — What is Accounts Receivable Aging and Why It Matters
**Chunk:** 41 · **Module:** Module 04 · **Duration target:** 3.5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** A business can be profitable and still run out of cash. The AR aging report tells you why.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** A business can be profitable and still run out of cash. The AR aging report tells you why.

**0:10 —** The aging report buckets every open invoice by how long it has gone unpaid: 0–30 days (current, on terms), 31–60, 61–90, and 90+ days (delinquent). As invoices age into the older buckets, the risk of non-collection rises and cash is tied up instead of in the bank.

**0:45 —** Here is Skarion's aging snapshot. Atlantic Fiber: $480,000, all current. Southeast Broadband: $405,000, half in the 31–60 bucket — watch it. TeleCom Builders: $112,700, split between 61–90 and 90+ days — this is the problem customer, and they get a dunning letter this week.

**1:30 —** Total AR is $997,700. Against $4.8M annual credit revenue, that's a DSO of about 75.8 days — it takes Skarion 76 days to collect a dollar of billed revenue. Controllers watch DSO month over month as a leading indicator of cash flow trouble.

**2:00 —** The aging report is how you turn AR from a number on a balance sheet into an action list: who to call, who to escalate, and which balances to add to the allowance for doubtful accounts.

# Video 34 — The 3-Way Match — Why You Never Pay an Invoice on Faith
**Chunk:** 34 · **Module:** Module 03 · **Duration target:** 4 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** One of the most powerful internal controls in AP — and one of the most common interview questions. Here is exactly how it works.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** The 3-way match is one of the most powerful internal controls in accounts payable. And it is one of the most common interview questions for any entry-level accounting role.

**0:30 —** You need three documents to agree before you can pay a vendor invoice: the Purchase Order (your company authorized the spend), the Receiving Report (the goods/services were actually delivered), and the Vendor Invoice (the amount billed matches what was ordered and delivered). All three numbers have to align on quantity, unit price, and total. Where all three overlap: pay it. Where only two overlap: hold it.

**1:30 —** A real example from Skarion's AP files. Invoice GCS-2026-087 from Global CAD Solutions. The MSA rate was $38.50 per mile. But the invoice billed $42.00 per mile — a $178.75 overage on 50 miles. The right move: hold the invoice and request a credit memo. Do not pay it.

**2:30 —** The 3-way match catches vendor billing errors, pricing changes that slipped through, duplicate invoices, and in worst cases, fraud. It only works if you actually do it on every invoice — not just the big ones.

**3:20 —** In the exercise coming up, you will get a batch of invoices and decide: pay, hold, or escalate. Use the 3-way match. The answer is in the documents.

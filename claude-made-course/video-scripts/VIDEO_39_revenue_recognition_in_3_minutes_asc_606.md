# Video 39 — Revenue Recognition in 3 Minutes (ASC 606)
**Chunk:** 39 · **Module:** Module 04 · **Duration target:** 3 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** A client signs a $2.4M contract. When do you record the $2.4M? The wrong answer costs companies millions in restatements.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** A client signs a $2.4 million contract. When do you record the $2.4M of revenue? The wrong answer costs companies millions in restatements, so listen carefully.

**0:20 —** ASC 606 is a five-step model. Identify the contract. Identify the distinct performance obligations — for a fiber design that's HLD, LLD, and a splice/permit package, three separate obligations. Determine the transaction price. Allocate that price to each obligation. Then recognize revenue when (or as) each obligation is satisfied.

**1:00 —** For Skarion's $2.4M contract over 3,000 miles: the $2.4M is not revenue at signing. It's not revenue when cash arrives. It becomes revenue as each deliverable is accepted — 40% at HLD delivery, 40% at LLD, 20% at the final permit package.

**1:40 —** Two balance-sheet accounts keep this honest. Deferred Revenue is cash received before work is done — a liability, not revenue. Unbilled AR (WIP) is work done but not yet at a billing milestone — an asset, value already created. Both exist precisely because "when cash moves" is the wrong question.

**2:20 —** Get this wrong and you overstate revenue. Get it right and your financials match the economics of the contract. That's the entire point of ASC 606.

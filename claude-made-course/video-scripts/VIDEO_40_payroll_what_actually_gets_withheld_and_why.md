# Video 40 — Payroll — What Actually Gets Withheld and Why
**Chunk:** 40 · **Module:** Module 03 · **Duration target:** 3.5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Gross pay is what the employee earns. Net pay is what they take home. The difference is your problem.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** Gross pay is what the employee earns. Net pay is what they take home. The difference is your problem — you have to withhold it, remit it, and account for it correctly.

**0:15 —** There are four big things withheld from a W2 employee's paycheck. Federal income tax — based on the W-4 the employee filed. FICA, which is Social Security (6.2% up to a wage cap) plus Medicare (1.45%, no cap). State income tax where applicable. And any voluntary deductions like 401(k) or health premiums.

**1:00 —** The employer also pays a matching FICA — another 7.65% — plus FUTA and SUTA unemployment. So on Skarion's $79,167 monthly gross payroll, employee withholdings total $21,056, employer taxes add $6,256, and $58,111 is the net cash wired to employees.

**2:00 —** The full entry: Debit Salaries Expense $79,167 and Payroll Tax Expense $6,256; Credit Payroll Tax Liabilities $27,312 and Credit Cash $58,111. The liabilities get cleared later when the tax deposits are made.

**3:00 —** 1099 contractors are different. No withholding, no employer FICA. You just pay the invoice through Accounts Payable. That's why misclassifying a W2 worker as a 1099 creates real IRS exposure — the company is skipping withholdings it legally owed.

**3:40 —** In the payroll entry lab next, you will post this full compound entry yourself.

# Video 91 — The 8 Ratios Every Accountant Must Know Cold
**Chunk:** 91 · **Module:** Module 10 · **Duration target:** 5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** A controller doesn't just close the books — they read them. These 8 ratios are how senior accountants immediately know whether a month was actually good or just looks good on the surface.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** A controller doesn't just close the books. They read them. These 8 ratios are how senior accountants immediately know whether a month was actually good or just looks good on the surface.

**0:30 —** The 8, with Skarion's January numbers: Gross Profit Margin (Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue = ($480K − $140K) ÷ $480K = 70.8% — how profitable the core service is before overhead. Operating Profit Margin = Operating Income ÷ Revenue = $242K ÷ $480K = 50.4% — after all operating costs. Current Ratio = Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities = $1.6M ÷ $340K = 4.7x — can the company pay short-term bills? >2x is healthy. Quick Ratio = (Cash + AR) ÷ Current Liabilities = ($336K + $997K) ÷ $340K = 3.9x — more conservative, excludes prepaids/inventory.

**1:30 —** DSO = AR ÷ (Revenue ÷ 365 days) = $997K ÷ ($4.8M ÷ 365) = 75.8 days — how long to collect; >60 flags a cash risk. DPO = AP ÷ (COGS ÷ 365) = $280K ÷ ($1.68M ÷ 365) = 60.8 days — how long before you pay vendors. Debt-to-Equity = Total Liabilities ÷ Total Equity = $820K ÷ $2.1M = 0.39x — leverage; <1x is conservative. Return on Assets = Net Income ÷ Total Assets = $242K ÷ $4.02M = 6.0% — how efficiently assets generate profit.

**2:30 —** Here is the real skill: knowing when a ratio changes and why. Gross margin dropped from 70.8% to 65.2% between January and February. That tells you COGS went up faster than revenue — before you even look at the detail. The ratio is the canary; the detail is the mine.

**4:00 —** In the lab, you'll calculate all 8 ratios from Skarion's Q1 data and write one sentence of interpretation for each. That sentence is the start of your controller's commentary.

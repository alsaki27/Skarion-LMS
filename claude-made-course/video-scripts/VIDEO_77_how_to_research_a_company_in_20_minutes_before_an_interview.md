# Video 77 — How to Research a Company in 20 Minutes Before an Interview
**Chunk:** 77 · **Module:** Module 09 · **Duration target:** 3 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Walk through the 20-minute framework on screen — Google News, the LinkedIn company page, Glassdoor, and the JD side by side.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** The 20 minutes before an interview matter more than the last week of cramming. Here's the framework.

**0:10 —** Ten minutes on the company: industry (how does accounting differ there?), size (team of 2 or a 40-person department?), recent news (Google "company name news 2025" — raises, acquisitions, layoffs, new contracts), Glassdoor reviews (what does the accounting team culture sound like?), and fiscal year-end (non-December close = no holiday slam).

**0:50 —** Five minutes on the role: what functions does the JD emphasize (AP, AR, close, payroll)? Match your strongest stories to those. What software — if it's NetSuite/Sage/SAP, Google how it differs from QuickBooks so you can speak about the learning curve. How much autonomy — "supports the controller" means oversight; "owns reconciliations" means independence.

**1:20 —** Five minutes on your interviewer: LinkedIn background — public accounting or industry? How senior? If they're the controller, they go technical. If HR, behavioral. Prepare accordingly.

**1:45 —** The three things to say in the first five minutes that prove you did your homework: a recent company news item, a specific phrase from their JD tied to your experience, and a question only someone who'd read their last 10-K / company page would ask. Research is preparation, not flattery.

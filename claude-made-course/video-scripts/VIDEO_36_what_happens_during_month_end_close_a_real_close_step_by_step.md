# Video 36 — What Happens During Month-End Close — A Real Close, Step by Step
**Chunk:** 36 · **Module:** Module 06 · **Duration target:** 5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** Month-end close is when accounting stops being data entry and becomes a high-stakes deadline operation. Everything in the right order, on the right day, nothing skipped.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** Month-end close is when accounting stops being an ongoing data-entry job and becomes a high-stakes deadline operation. Everything has to be done in the right order, on the right day, and nothing can be skipped.

**0:30 —** Here is Skarion's five-day close schedule. Day one: lock all timesheets and AP — any invoice not entered by 5 PM goes into next month. Day two: post payroll, employer taxes, and all offshore contractor accruals. Day three: calculate WIP, post depreciation, post the loan interest accrual. Day four: bank reconciliation, tie the AR subledger to the GL, tie the AP subledger to the GL. Day five: review the P&amp;L against budget, produce the financial package, get controller sign-off.

**2:00 —** The hardest part for a services firm is the adjusting entries — entries for things that happened but haven't been invoiced yet. Your offshore drafting team worked 400 hours in January. The invoice won't arrive until February 4th. But the expense belongs in January. So on January 31st, you accrue it: debit COGS Subcontractors $14,800, credit Accrued Liabilities $14,800.

**3:00 —** Then on February 1st, you reverse that accrual. When the invoice actually arrives on the 4th, you post it normally. The reversal ensures the expense doesn't get double-counted.

**4:00 —** The close playbook in your course materials lists every entry you need to post during Skarion's January close. Work through it in SkarionBooks — the right order, the right amounts, every one.

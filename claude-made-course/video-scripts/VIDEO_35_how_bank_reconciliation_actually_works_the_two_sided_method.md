# Video 35 — How Bank Reconciliation Actually Works — The Two-Sided Method
**Chunk:** 35 · **Module:** Module 05 · **Duration target:** 5 min · **Type:** Talking head / instructor
**Learning objective:** 'Walk me through a bank reconciliation' is the most common technical question in entry-level accounting interviews. After this, you'll have a real answer with a real dollar amount.

## Narration (transcript fallback exists in the in-course `video` chunk)

**0:00 —** "Walk me through a bank reconciliation." This is the most common technical question in entry-level accounting interviews. After this video, you will have a real answer with a real dollar amount — not a textbook definition.

**0:30 —** A bank reconciliation proves your books and the bank's records show the same true cash balance, after accounting for timing differences. The bank doesn't know about the check you wrote yesterday. Your books don't know about the $18.50 of interest the bank credited this morning. These are not errors — they are expected.

**1:30 —** The method, both directions. Bank side: start with the ending balance, add deposits in transit, subtract outstanding checks, that's the adjusted bank balance. Book side: start with the GL balance, add bank credits not yet recorded, subtract bank fees not yet recorded, that's the adjusted book balance. These two adjusted numbers must be identical. If they aren't, there is a real error — and finding it is the job.

**3:00 —** In Skarion's January reconciliation, the bank showed $312,450 and the books showed $346,823.60. Four items were timing differences, and one was a real error: a $10,547.10 customer deposit had been entered twice in the GL. The correcting entry: debit Accounts Receivable, credit Cash. The reconciliation then balanced to exactly $336,250.

**4:00 —** Notice we did not debit Sales Revenue — the sale was never overstated. We only debit AR, because the cash side was overstated. Details like that make an interviewer actually believe you.

**4:30 —** In the lab, you will reconcile three months of Skarion's bank statements. Each one has a seeded error. Find it, fix it, document it.

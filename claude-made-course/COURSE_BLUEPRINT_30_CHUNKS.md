## Module 04 — Accounts Receivable & Billing
**Tag:** Mod 04 · **Chunks:** 13–16 · **Tone:** Revenue-focused, ASC 606 grounded

### Chunk 13 — Revenue Recognition & ASC 606
**Type:** Content + Timeline
- **ASC 606 — The 5-Step Model:**
  1. Identify the contract with the customer
  2. Identify the distinct performance obligations (e.g., HLD delivery, LLD delivery, splice package)
  3. Determine the transaction price
  4. Allocate the transaction price to each performance obligation
  5. Recognize revenue when (or as) each obligation is satisfied
- Why this matters: Skarion signs a $2.4M contract for a 3,000-mile fiber design. When is the $2.4M revenue recognized? Not when the contract is signed. Not when the cash arrives. When each deliverable is completed.
- **Timeline:** Contract signing → HLD delivery (recognize 40%) → LLD delivery (recognize 40%) → Splice/Permit package delivery (recognize 20%)
- Deferred Revenue: when the client pays in advance before work is done — it's a liability, not revenue
- Unbilled AR (WIP): when the work is done but the milestone hasn't triggered yet

### Chunk 14 — Creating the AR Invoice & Recording It

## Module 05 — Bank Reconciliation & Cash Posting
**Tag:** Mod 05 · **Chunks:** 17–20 · **Tone:** Methodical, zero tolerance for shortcuts

### Chunk 17 — Why the Bank Balance Never Matches the Books (And Shouldn't)
**Type:** Content + Two-Sided Reconciliation Table
- Timing differences are not errors — they are expected: deposits in transit, outstanding checks, bank fees, interest credits
- The two-sided reconciliation methodology:
  - **Bank Side:** Statement balance + Deposits in Transit − Outstanding Checks = Adjusted Bank Balance
  - **Book Side:** GL balance + Bank Credits not yet recorded − Bank Fees/Debits not yet recorded = Adjusted Book Balance
  - **Required Result:** Adjusted Bank Balance = Adjusted Book Balance — always, exactly
- If they don't equal after timing adjustments: there is a real error. Find it before you touch anything.
- **Skarion Real Scenario:** January statement shows $312,450. GL shows $301,848. The $10,602 gap is not one thing — it's four timing differences plus one real error.

### Chunk 18 — Cash Posting: Applying Client Payments to Open AR
**Type:** Content + Journal Entry Builder
- What cash posting actually is: matching incoming cash receipts to specific open AR invoices and clearing them
- Why this is not automatic: clients often pay late, partially, combine multiple invoices in one wire, or deduct credits/disputes without notice
- **The cash posting workflow:**
  1. Receive bank notification of incoming wire ($480,000 from Atlantic Fiber Co.)
  2. Open AR subledger — identify the open invoice(s) that match
  3. Confirm remittance advice from client (they should send this — chase it if they don't)
  4. Post the receipt: Dr Cash / Cr AR — and close the specific invoice in the subledger
  5. If the amount doesn't exactly match: document the short payment, issue a dispute, or credit the difference with manager approval
- **Wire Transfer with Bank Fee — Journal Entry Builder:**
  - Atlantic Fiber Co. wires $480,000. Bank charges a $25 incoming wire fee. Net deposit: $479,975.
  - Entry: Dr Operating Checking $479,975 + Dr Bank Fees Expense $25 / Cr Accounts Receivable $480,000
- **Partial Payment Scenario:**
  - Southeast Broadband pays only $190,000 of their $405,000 balance with a note that the remainder is "in dispute."
  - Entry: Dr Cash $190,000 / Cr AR $190,000 (apply to oldest invoice, document the dispute on the remaining balance)
- **NSF Check Scenario:**
  - TeleCom Builders sends a $50,000 check. It bounces. The bank reverses the deposit and charges a $35 NSF fee.
  - Entry: Dr AR $50,000 + Dr Bank Fees $35 / Cr Cash $50,035 (reinstate the receivable, record the fee)

### Chunk 19 — Bank Reconciliation: Finding and Fixing Real Errors
**Type:** Flip Cards + Journal Entry Builder
- **Flip Cards — The 6 most common reconciliation problems:**
  1. **Duplicate deposit:** Same customer payment entered twice in GL — Dr AR / Cr Cash to reverse the duplicate
  2. **Outstanding check:** Check issued and recorded but vendor hasn't cashed it yet — subtract from bank side
  3. **Amount transposition:** Rent recorded as $12,750 but actually cleared at $12,800 — $50 correcting entry
  4. **Bank fee not recorded:** Monthly service charge, wire fee, or NSF fee hit the bank but never entered in books
  5. **Deposit in transit:** Last-day deposit recorded in books but not yet on bank statement
  6. **Fraudulent transaction:** Unauthorized ACH debit — immediately flagged, bank dispute filed, Dr AR or Due from Bank / Cr Cash
- **Skarion January Reconciliation — Full Journal Entry Builder:**
  - Bank statement: $312,450.00
  - Deposits in transit: +$28,000.00 (payment received Dec 31 not on statement)
  - Outstanding check #2847: −$4,200.00 (payable to Global CAD Solutions)
  - Adjusted Bank Balance: **$336,250.00**
  - GL balance: $346,823.60
  - Unrecorded bank fee: −$45.00
  - Unrecorded interest: +$18.50
  - Duplicate deposit (DEP-0091, Southeast Broadband): −$10,547.10
  - Adjusted Book Balance: **$336,250.00** ✓
  - Correcting entry for duplicate: Dr AR $10,547.10 / Cr Cash $10,547.10
  - Correcting entry for bank fee: Dr Bank Fees Expense $45 / Cr Cash $45
  - Correcting entry for interest: Dr Cash $18.50 / Cr Interest Income $18.50

## Module 06 — Month-End Close & Financial Statements
**Tag:** Mod 06 · **Chunks:** 21–23 · **Tone:** Systems-thinking, deadline-driven

### Chunk 21 — The Month-End Close Process
**Type:** Timeline + Content Table
- Why month-end close exists: financial statements are produced once per period; they must be accurate, complete, and timely
- The close checklist approach: every task has an owner, a due date, a status, and supporting documentation
- **Skarion's 5-Day Close Timeline:**
  - Day 1 (Feb 1): Lock timesheets, lock AP, generate AP aging, post all outstanding invoices
  - Day 2 (Feb 2): Post payroll entry and employer taxes, book all offshore contractor accruals, amortize prepaids
  - Day 3 (Feb 3): Calculate WIP, post depreciation, post loan interest accrual, run trial balance
  - Day 4 (Feb 4): Bank reconciliation, tie AR subledger to GL, tie AP subledger to GL
  - Day 5 (Feb 5): Review P&L vs. prior month and budget, produce financial statement package, get controller sign-off
- Key concept: **hard close vs. soft close** — hard close locks the period permanently; soft close allows corrections up to the audit

### Chunk 22 — Adjusting Entries: The Heart of Month-End
**Type:** Content + Journal Entry Builder (5 scenarios)
- Why adjusting entries exist: the accrual principle requires recording things that haven't generated paperwork yet
- The 4 types of adjusting entries:
  1. **Accrued Revenues** (Unbilled AR / WIP)
  2. **Accrued Expenses** (offshore work done, invoice not yet received)
  3. **Deferred Revenues** (cash received for future work)
  4. **Deferred Expenses** (prepaids being consumed)
- **Skarion Journal Entry Builder — 5 Adjusting Entries:**

## SCORM & Technical Packaging Notes

### Architecture
- **Single SPA build:** One `index.html` compiles all 9 modules via `build_course.py`. No inter-module page loads.
- **Left-nav sidebar:** Collapsible by module. Each submodule listed. Active chunk highlighted. Scrollable with `overflow-y: auto`.

- **Chunk satisfaction rules:**
  - `content` chunks: auto-satisfied on scroll-to-end

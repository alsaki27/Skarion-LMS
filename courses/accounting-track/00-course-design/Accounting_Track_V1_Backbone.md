# PART 2: THE COURSE PHILOSOPHY

## "Simulate a Year, Don't Teach a Semester"

Traditional accounting courses teach concepts: "This is what accrual accounting means. This is the accounting equation."

Our course does the opposite: **The candidate BECOMES the accountant for a fictional company for 12 simulated months.** They process every transaction, close every month, reconcile every account, and produce every report.

By the end, they can say: **"I handled the full accounting cycle for a $2M-revenue company. I processed 200+ invoices, reconciled 12 months of bank statements, posted 150+ journal entries, and prepared monthly financial reports."**

That is indistinguishable from 1 year of real experience.

### The Fictional Company: "Skarion Manufacturing LLC"

We create a fictional company that candidates "work for" throughout the course:
- **Type:** Small manufacturing company (relatable, common, shows inventory/cost understanding)
- **Revenue:** $2M annually
- **Employees:** 15 people
- **Location:** Virginia (matches Skarion's location)
- **Accounting System:** QuickBooks Online (free trial for candidates)
- **Transactions:** 150-200 per month, realistic business scenarios
- **Bank:** Monthly bank statements provided (we create these)
- **Vendors:** 20 regular vendors with realistic invoices
- **Customers:** 30 regular customers with realistic sales
- **Employees:** Payroll with tax withholdings, benefits, PTO
- **Loan:** Business loan with monthly payments
- **Equipment:** Fixed assets with depreciation

### Why This Works

When an interviewer asks: "Tell me about a month-end close you've done."

The candidate says: "At my previous company, Skarion Manufacturing, I handled the full month-end close. Each month, I reconciled 4 bank accounts, posted accruals for utilities and salaries, recorded depreciation, reconciled AR and AP aging, and prepared the P&L and balance sheet. The close typically took 3 days. For example, in March, I found a $1,200 discrepancy in the bank reconciliation — a deposit was recorded twice. I traced it to the source, reversed the duplicate entry, and documented the correction. That experience taught me to always check deposit dates against the bank statement before posting."

**This is a TRUE story about a SIMULATED experience.** The candidate BELIEVES it because they DID it. The interviewer believes it because it has specific details (4 bank accounts, $1,200 discrepancy, March).

# PART 3: THE ESSENTIAL PATH (10-Day Intensive)

## Goal: Candidate can confidently say "I have 1 year of staff accounting experience."

---

### DAY 1: US Accounting Foundations & QuickBooks Setup
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- What is US GAAP? (not IFRS — candidates must know this is US-specific)
- Cash vs. Accrual accounting (most US companies use accrual)
- The accounting equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
- Chart of accounts: what it is, how it's structured, how to read it
- Debits and credits: the REAL way (not just "left and right")
  - Assets = Debit to increase
  - Liabilities = Credit to increase
  - Equity = Credit to increase
  - Revenue = Credit to increase
  - Expenses = Debit to increase
- Common accounts: Cash, AR, AP, Inventory, Fixed Assets, Accumulated Depreciation, Revenue, COGS, Rent, Salaries, Utilities, Loans

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- QuickBooks Online: Setting up the company file
- Company settings: fiscal year, tax form, accounting method
- Chart of accounts setup and customization
- Connecting (or simulating) bank accounts
- Importing opening balances
- User roles and permissions
- Navigating the dashboard: Banking, Sales, Expenses, Reports

**Homework:** Create the Skarion Manufacturing company in QuickBooks. Set up the chart of accounts. Document the setup in a "New Hire Onboarding" memo.

---

### DAY 2: Daily Transactions — AP & Expenses
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- The AP process: Receive invoice → Verify → Approve → Enter → Pay
- 3-way match: Purchase Order + Receiving Report + Invoice
- Entering vendor bills in QuickBooks
- Recording expenses: utilities, rent, office supplies, professional services
- Billable vs. non-billable expenses
- Entering and reimbursing employee expenses (Concur-style workflow)
- Petty cash management

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Process 30 vendor invoices for Skarion Manufacturing
- Match invoices to POs and receiving reports
- Enter bills, categorize expenses, assign to classes/locations if applicable
- Practice: "An invoice from ABC Supply for $2,400 in raw materials arrived. The PO was for $2,200. What do you do?"
- Documenting discrepancies and vendor communications

**Homework:** Complete all January vendor bills. Create an AP aging report.

---

### DAY 3: Daily Transactions — AR & Sales
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- The AR process: Quote → Order → Invoice → Payment → Reconcile
- Creating customer invoices in QuickBooks
- Sales tax setup and calculation (Virginia sales tax rules)
- Recording sales receipts vs. invoices
- Payment terms: Net 30, Net 15, 2/10 Net 30
- Early payment discounts
- Recording deposits
- Credit memos and returns

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Create and send 25 customer invoices for January sales
- Record customer payments (checks, ACH, credit cards)
- Apply payments to specific invoices (cash application)
- Handle partial payments and overpayments
- Create an AR aging report
- Practice: "A customer paid $4,800 but the invoice was $5,000. What do you do?"

**Homework:** Complete all January invoicing and cash posting. Create an AR aging report. Reconcile AR subledger to GL.

---

### DAY 4: Banking & Reconciliation
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- Bank feeds in QuickBooks: connecting and categorizing
- Bank rules: setting up automatic categorization
- Recording transfers between accounts
- Recording loan payments (principal vs. interest split)
- Handling bank fees and interest income
- Petty cash reconciliation
- Credit card reconciliation

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Reconcile January bank statement for Skarion Manufacturing
- Step-by-step: Match transactions, investigate discrepancies, clear items
- Common reconciliation issues and fixes:
  - Duplicate transactions
  - Missing transactions
  - Wrong amounts
  - Outstanding checks
  - Deposits in transit
- **Practice problem:** Bank statement shows $12,450. QuickBooks shows $12,180. Find the $270 discrepancy.

**Homework:** Complete January bank reconciliation. Write a reconciliation memo explaining any discrepancies found and resolved.

---

### DAY 5: Payroll & Employee Costs
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- Payroll accounting basics
- Gross pay, net pay, tax withholdings (Federal, State, FICA, Medicare)
- Employer payroll taxes (FUTA, SUTA, matching FICA)
- Recording payroll journal entries
- PTO accruals and tracking
- Benefits and 401k deductions
- Payroll reconciliation (payroll register to GL)

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Process January payroll for Skarion Manufacturing (15 employees)
- Calculate withholdings manually (understand the mechanics)
- Enter payroll in QuickBooks
- Record employer payroll tax expense
- Reconcile payroll register to bank withdrawal
- Create payroll summary report

**Homework:** Complete January payroll. Document all payroll journal entries.

---

### DAY 6: Fixed Assets & Depreciation
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- What are fixed assets? (equipment, vehicles, furniture, computers)
- Capitalization vs. expense (GAAP rules: over $2,500 typically capitalized)
- Depreciation methods: Straight-line (most common), declining balance
- Useful lives: Equipment (5-7 years), computers (3 years), furniture (7 years)
- Accumulated depreciation and book value
- Disposals and gains/losses
- Asset register maintenance

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Record fixed asset purchases for Skarion Manufacturing
- Calculate monthly depreciation for 10 assets
- Post depreciation journal entries
- Reconcile fixed asset register to GL
- Practice: "A computer was purchased for $3,000. Calculate monthly depreciation. After 18 months, it was sold for $1,500. What is the gain/loss?"

**Homework:** Complete fixed asset entries for January. Update asset register.

---

### DAY 7: Month-End Close — Part 1 (Accruals & Adjustments)
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- What is month-end close? Why does it matter?
- The close checklist: 15 steps every accountant follows
- Accrual entries: salaries, utilities, interest, taxes
- Prepaid expenses: amortization (insurance, rent paid in advance)
- Unearned revenue: when cash is received before work is done
- Depreciation (already covered, but included in close)
- Inventory adjustments (if applicable)
- Bad debt allowance

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Perform January month-end close for Skarion Manufacturing
- Post all accrual journal entries
- Record prepaid amortization
- Adjust inventory (if count differs from books)
- Review trial balance for reasonableness
- Investigate any unusual variances

**Homework:** Complete all January adjusting entries. Prepare adjusted trial balance.

---

### DAY 8: Month-End Close — Part 2 (Reconciliation & Reporting)
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- Reconciling all balance sheet accounts:
  - Bank accounts (already done, but final review)
  - AR subledger to GL
  - AP subledger to GL
  - Inventory to GL
  - Fixed assets to GL
  - Loan balances to statements
  - Payroll liabilities
  - Sales tax payable
- Intercompany reconciliations (if applicable)
- Reviewing the trial balance: Debits = Credits?
- Preparing the financial statements

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Complete January close
- Generate P&L (Income Statement)
- Generate Balance Sheet
- Generate Cash Flow Statement (QuickBooks auto-generates, but understand it)
- Prepare management report package:
  - Executive summary (2 paragraphs)
  - Key metrics: Revenue, Gross Profit, Net Income, Cash position
  - Variance vs. budget (if budget exists)
  - AR aging summary
  - AP aging summary
- **Practice:** Write a 1-page "January Financial Summary" for the CEO

**Homework:** Complete January close package. Save all reports.

### DAY 9: Excel for Accounting (The Real Toolkit)
**Morning Session (2 hours):**
- Why Excel is the #1 tool for accountants (even with QuickBooks)
- VLOOKUP and INDEX-MATCH (matching data between tables)
- SUMIFS, COUNTIFS (conditional calculations)
- Pivot tables: summarizing GL data, creating reports
- IF statements and nested formulas
- Text functions: CONCATENATE, LEFT, RIGHT, TEXT
- Date functions: EOMONTH, DATEDIF, TODAY
- Data validation and dropdown lists
- Conditional formatting for flagging issues
- Protecting worksheets and workbooks

**Afternoon Session (2 hours):**
- Hands-on: Build 3 Excel tools from scratch:
  1. **Bank Reconciliation Worksheet:** Import bank statement + GL, match transactions, highlight discrepancies
  2. **AR Aging Analysis:** Import AR data, calculate aging buckets (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+), identify overdue accounts
  3. **Expense Analysis Dashboard:** Import expense data, categorize, create pivot tables, build charts showing spending by category and vendor

**Homework:** Complete all 3 Excel tools. Submit as portfolio pieces.

### DAY 10: Year-End & Advanced Topics
**Morning Session (2 hours):**

- 1099 preparation: who gets one, how to file

- Accrued vacation/year-end adjustments

- Prepare quarterly management report

# PART 5: MINIMUM VIABLE READINESS (MVR) FOR ACCOUNTING TRACK

A candidate is "Accounting MVR-ready" when they can:

1. **Explain** the difference between cash and accrual accounting, and why US companies use accrual
2. **Set up** a company in QuickBooks Online with a proper chart of accounts
3. **Process** AP: enter vendor bills, match to POs, handle discrepancies, process payments
4. **Process** AR: create invoices, record payments, apply cash, handle partial payments, create aging reports
5. **Reconcile** a bank statement to the general ledger and explain any discrepancies found
6. **Process** payroll: calculate withholdings, record journal entries, reconcile to bank
7. **Post** month-end journal entries: accruals, prepaids, depreciation, adjustments
8. **Generate** a complete set of financial statements: P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow
9. **Use** Excel for accounting: VLOOKUP, pivot tables, SUMIFS, bank reconciliation worksheet, AR aging
10. **Tell** a specific story about "their" month-end close with details, numbers, and lessons learned

### MVR does NOT require:
- CPA certification
- Tax return preparation (beyond basic 1099 understanding)
- Advanced financial modeling (DCF, valuation)
- Audit experience (though they should understand what auditors ask for)
- Multi-entity consolidation
- International accounting (IFRS)
- Public company reporting (SEC filings)

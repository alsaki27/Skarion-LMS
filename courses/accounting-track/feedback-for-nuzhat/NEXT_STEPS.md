# What's Missing & What Nuzhat Should Do Next

Beyond fixing the existing files (see `FEEDBACK_FOR_NUZHAT.md`), here is what the course still needs to fulfill the backbone's promise and the Skarion model — teach on-the-job tasks, groom interactively on projects, place with that experience.

## Tier 1 — Blocks launch (do these after the fixes)

### 1. Assignment 10 was never done: competitor comparison
The backbone's Assignment 10 (compare vs. community college certificate, Udemy course, bootcamp — table + "what makes Skarion different" brief) has no deliverable in the folder. This is also the marketing backbone for selling the course. **Est. 2 hours.**

### 2. AR-side materials don't exist
The course has 30 AP invoices but **zero customer-side artifacts**: no sales invoices to create, no customer payments to apply, no cash-application exercise with partial/over-payments, no collections scenario. Day 3 of the 10-day path currently has nothing to practice on. Build the AR mirror of Assignment 5: ~25 customer invoices + a remittance batch (checks/ACH with 2–3 short-pays and 1 overpayment) + a collections email exercise. **Est. 4–5 hours — this is the single biggest content hole.**

### 3. February and March transaction packets
Bank statements exist for Feb/Mar, but the bills/invoices/payroll data to *produce* those months only exist for January (Assignment 3). Day 10 requires an "accelerated Feb+Mar" — students need the source transactions. **Est. 4 hours** (they can be lighter than January's packet).

### 4. The lesson-content layer
Everything built so far is *assessment/practice* material. The teaching layer — slide decks or scripted video walkthroughs for each morning session (GAAP basics, debits/credits, QBO navigation, payroll mechanics, close process) — doesn't exist yet. The backbone's Day 1–10 morning outlines are the syllabus; each needs ~60–90 min of teachable content. **This is the next major build phase. Est. 2–3 weeks.**

## Tier 2 — Should exist before first cohort

5. **Grading/completion rubrics per module** — A9 and A6 have rubrics; the other modules need pass/fail criteria so MVR ("minimum viable readiness") is actually measurable. The 10-point MVR checklist in the backbone should become a scored exit assessment.
6. **A capstone script** — the backbone's portfolio list (8 artifacts) exists, but there's no student-facing instruction doc: "here is exactly what you export, in what format, named how, into what folder." Employers may ask to see it; it should look uniform across candidates.
7. **Mock-interview protocol** — the question bank exists; what's missing is the *process*: who plays interviewer, how sessions are scored against the rubric, how many rounds before placement-ready.
8. **AI-in-accounting lesson + interview answers** (see `JOB_MARKET_RESEARCH_2026.md` §3) — position graduates as automation-fluent, not data-entry-only.
9. **Bill.com + XLOOKUP micro-lessons** — both now appear in postings; both are cheap to add.

## Tier 3 — Improvements over time

10. Expand the 12-month simulation (months 4–12 currently exist only as a table in the backbone).
11. A "QBO sandbox reset" guide — trials expire in 30 days; students mid-course need a documented recovery path (export → new trial → reimport).
12. Convert the interview cheat sheet into flashcard/quiz format for the LMS.
13. Track placement outcomes per cohort and feed the real interview questions students get back into the question bank.

## Suggested order of operations for Nuzhat

1. Week 1: Fixes from FEEDBACK_FOR_NUZHAT.md items 1–4 (redo A2, solution-key errors, template bug, rebuild A1).
2. Week 1–2: Master company profile alignment pass + Skarion-ify interview answers.
3. Week 2–3: Build the AR packet (item 2 above) and Feb/Mar transactions (item 3).
4. Week 3: Assignment 10 comparison brief + capstone script.
5. Week 4+: Begin the lesson-content layer, one day-module at a time, in the order of the 10-day path.
